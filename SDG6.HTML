SDG - 6 & Indian Traditions


**Water Conservation**

:

Embrace the traditional practice of conserving water in everyday life. Turn off taps when not in use, fix leaks promptly, and avoid wasteful water practices.


**Rainwater Harvesting**

: Learn about and promote rainwater harvesting techniques in your community. Collecting rainwater can help recharge groundwater and provide an additional source of clean water.

**River Cleanliness**

:

participate in or organise river cleaning drives to prevent pollution and maintain the cleanliness of water bodies.

**Support NGOs**

:

Get involved with local NGOs and organisations working on water and sanitation projects. Your time and effort can make a difference.

**Community Wells**

:

f your community has traditional wells, take part in efforts to restore and maintain them. These wells often serve as valuable sources of clean water.

**Promote Hygiene**

:

Encourage proper hygiene practices, such as regular handwashing, in your school and community to prevent waterborne diseases.

**Advocate for Sanitation**

:

Raise awareness about the importance of sanitation facilities, especially in rural areas, and support initiatives to improve access to clean and safe toilets.

**Philanthropy**

:

Cultivate a spirit of giving and philanthropy. Even small contributions can make a difference in the lives of those in need, and philanthropy is a key element of Indian traditions.

***Reduce Plastic Pollution**

:

Recognize the connection between plastic pollution and water contamination. Reduce single-use plastic consumption and participate in plastic cleanup drives near water bodies.
