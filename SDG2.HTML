SDG - 2 & Indian Traditions


**Vegetarianism**

:

Learn and practice gardening at home or in your community. Growing your own vegetables and herbs promotes sustainable agriculture and can reduce the need for processed and imported foods.


**Gardening**

: Get involved in community service and engagement. Indian traditions often emphasise the importance of giving back to the community, which aligns with the idea of fostering partnerships for development.

**Food Preservation**

:

Learn traditional methods of food preservation, such as pickling and drying, to reduce food waste and extend the shelf life of produce.




**Composting**

:

Composting kitchen scraps and organic waste can enrich the soil, reducing the need for chemical fertilisers and contributing to sustainable agriculture.

**Support Local Farmers**

:

Buy locally grown and organic produce to support local farmers and reduce the carbon footprint associated with long-distance food transportation.




**Traditional Cooking**

:

Learn traditional Indian recipes that use locally sourced, seasonal ingredients. Traditional Indian cuisine often incorporates diverse grains, legumes, and vegetables.

**Seed Saving**

:

Explore the practice of saving seeds from your garden or local crops. Seed saving helps preserve heirloom varieties and biodiversity.development.

**Community Initiatives**

:

Participate in or initiate community gardens, farmers' markets, or food co-ops to promote sustainable agriculture and increase access to fresh, locally produced food.

**Food Donations**

:

Engage in food donation drives or volunteer at food banks to help address hunger and food insecurity in your community.
